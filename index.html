Project Aim

We proposed a randomized control trial (RCT) study to investigate the impact of innovative Sultana’s reusable cloth pads (improved cloth pad made of flannel cloth and water proof barrier sheet) with appropriate laundering practices, on the incidence of reproductive tract infections and urinary tract infections (UTIs) in women. Based on the eight school and eight readymade garments factory pilot testing experiences, the uptake of the of Sultana reusable cloth pad was over 76% among both women and girls and it reduced absenteeism both in schools (by 8%) and in the RMGs (by 1%). The pad was comfortable, did not stain, soft and reusable throughout the assessments. The effectiveness of hygienic menstrual products will be assessed in reducing incidence of RTIs, UTIs, and contributing to the reduction of antibiotic use and subsequently the prevention of antimicrobial resistance (AMR), and elucidating the genetic mechanisms underlying AMR through whole genome sequencing (WGS) using the Illumina platform, in Bangladesh through a comprehensive research and data integration approach.

Objectives

Methodologies

•Investigate the effect of innovative reusable cloth pads including dryer and washer to prevent of RTIs and UTIs in women.

•A diverse group of women and adolescents from Bangladesh will provide baseline samples, collected by trained nurses and analyzed for RTIs and UTIs. Randomized intervention groups will receive reusable cloth pads, education via mobile apps, and regular data collection. Clinical data will assess the impact of cloth pad use on RTI and UTI prevention.

•Examine the effectiveness of hygienic menstrual products in reducing RTIs, UTIs, and thus contributing to the reduction of antibiotic use and AMR.

•We'll keep collecting data on RTI and UTI symptoms and specimens from both groups to assess hygienic menstrual product effectiveness in reducing incidence and uncover underlying causes.

•Characterize multi-drug resistant bacterial pathogens for antimicrobial resistance genes, virulence and phylogenetic diversity using WGS.

•Lab personnel will extract DNA and conduct Whole Genome Sequencing (WGS) to assess microbial diversity in urine, stool, and vaginal microbiomes. WGS identifies pathogen genetics, aiding in predicting disease severity, treatment outcomes, and drug-resistant strains. We'll also analyze genes related to antimicrobial resistance to understand resistance development.

•Assess the impact of web-based tools and mobile apps in educating and raising awareness about sustainable menstrual practices and AMR prevention and sharing real-time data into the national dashboard of the a2i (Aspire to Innovate).

•We'll develop a web tool with data entry and health education sessions featuring informative videos on our app. Training and education will focus on innovative hygienic product use. Real-time data on RTIs and UTIs, antibiotic use, and AMR status will go to the a2i database (https://a2i.gov.bd/), aiding decision-making and interventions, similar to its use for other viral infections.
